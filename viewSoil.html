Soil Information

1. Alluvial Soil:

Location: Found in the Indo-Gangetic plains, river valleys, and coastal areas.

Characteristics: Rich in fertility, well-drained, suitable for a variety of crops.

Crops: Rice, wheat, sugarcane, cotton, jute.

2. Black Soil (Regur Soil):

Location: Deccan Plateau, parts of Gujarat, Maharashtra, and Madhya Pradesh.

Characteristics: Retains moisture well, rich in iron and aluminum, becomes sticky when wet.

Crops: Cotton, sugarcane, pulses, oilseeds.

3. Red and Yellow Soil:

Location: Found in areas with a moderate rainfall, such as parts of Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and Tamil Nadu.

Characteristics: Rich in iron, low fertility, acidic in nature.

Crops: Millets, pulses, groundnut, tobacco.

4. Laterite Soil:

Location: Western Ghats, Eastern Ghats, and parts of Odisha and Jharkhand.

Characteristics: Poor fertility, rich in iron and aluminum oxides, leaches quickly.

Crops: Cashew, tea, coffee.

5. Mountain or Forest Soil:

Location: Hilly and forested areas in the Himalayan region and the Western Ghats.

Characteristics: High organic content, acidic, prone to erosion.

Crops: Tea, coffee, fruits, vegetables.

6. Arid and Desert Soil:

Location: Rajasthan, parts of Gujarat and Haryana.

Characteristics: Low fertility, high alkalinity, sandy in nature.

Crops: Millets, barley, pulses, oilseeds.

7. Peat Soil:

Location: Found in the coastal areas of Kerala and the Sundarbans in West Bengal.

Characteristics: Organic, high moisture content.

Crops: Rice, vegetables.
